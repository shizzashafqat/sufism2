For centuries, Qawwali lived in the heart of Sufi shrines: dim courtyards filled with candles, rose petals, langar, and the hum of devotees seeking comfort, healing, and divine love. Here, music was not performance, it was prayer. In these intimate Mehfil-e-Sama gatherings, qawwals sang to induce spiritual ecstasy (wajd), to bind communities together, and to carry forward the poetry of saints across generations.
